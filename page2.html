Alcohol

Alcohol is like a flickering light swaying in the late night, Silent, yet carving a quiet space for me on the glass. When the world's sounds grow too sharp, too close, It softens their edge into mist, turns pain into gentle tides. I know it's not the answer, yet in those suffocating moments, it offers its own fleeting warmth— like a weary soul seeking shelter in the rain, like the sigh of wind through old curtains. It's not the fire that makes me braver, just the light that makes me feel less alone for a while.
