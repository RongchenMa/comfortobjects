Cosmetics

Cosmetics lie quietly in the corner of the desk, like a row of silent guardians. As the morning light falls, I smooth away last night's weariness with foundation, and give myself a reason to keep going with lipstick. They are not merely colors, but thin armor shielding vulnerability, and the boundary where I let the world draw near. When the brush sweeps across my cheeks, it's not just adornment, but a gentle reassurance— a reminder: I still hold the reins. Amidst chaos, anxiety, and uncertainty, this hint of radiance becomes the rhythm of my breath. When the world grows too harsh, I use a layer of color to carve out a safe space for myself. =
